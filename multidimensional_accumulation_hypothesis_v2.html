Thesis

Conscious activity is a physical pattern instantiated in a biological brain. Every act of computation has thermodynamic, electromagnetic, and (possibly) quantum-coherent byproducts that leave traces in the environment. These traces are bounded in information content by Bekenstein-type entropy limits, propagate at finite speed, and do not disappear at biological death — they continue to exist as distributed informational structure in the surrounding physical universe.

We do not claim this distributed residue constitutes a continuing experiencing subject. We claim only that the informational pattern sufficient to specify a mind has non-trivial physical persistence after the brain ceases to instantiate it, and we attempt to quantify and test this claim.

Five Pillars

1Thermodynamic Necessity of Information Outflow

Any irreversible computation must dissipate at least kT ln 2 per erased bit (Landauer's principle). A 20W brain operating at body temperature dissipates this energy primarily as heat — but heat radiation is structured electromagnetic emission carrying information about its source.

E_min = k_B T · ln(2) ≈ 2.9 × 10⁻²¹ J / bit @ 310 K

Implication: a 20W brain dissipating ~ 6.9×10²¹ bits/sec worth of Landauer-equivalent energy must couple some fraction of that to the environment through radiative channels. The strict 100% confinement of v1 is thermodynamically impossible.

2Bioelectromagnetic Far-Field Emission

The brain produces measurable magnetic fields on the order of 10⁻¹³ – 10⁻¹² T at the scalp (MEG range), and far-field EM components — though minute — exist in principle. Once emitted, EM radiation propagates at c indefinitely in a vacuum-like ISM, carrying spectral signatures of its source.

B_brain ≈ 100 fT (alpha rhythm) P_emit ≈ small fraction of metabolic 20W

This is the only uncontroversially physical channel of "leakage". A sufficiently advanced observer with arbitrary sensitivity could, in principle, recover correlation structure of brain activity from this radiation indefinitely into the future.

3Quantum-Biological Channels (Speculative)

Penrose–Hameroff Orch-OR and Fisher's Posner-molecule proposal suggest non-trivial quantum coherence in neural substrate. Tegmark (2000) calculates decoherence times ~10⁻¹³ s, posing a serious objection. Fisher (2015) responds that nuclear spins in phosphate ions are far more isolated and may sustain coherence at biologically relevant timescales.

τ_decoherence ≈ 10⁻¹³ s (Tegmark 2000) τ_Posner ≈ 10⁵ s (Fisher 2015, ³¹P nuclear spin)

If even a weak quantum-coherent channel exists, environmental entanglement provides a second persistence mechanism beyond classical EM. We flag this as speculative, not load-bearing.

4Holographic Information Bound

By the Bekenstein bound and the holographic principle ('t Hooft 1993, Susskind 1995), the total information content of a region of space is bounded by its enclosing surface area in Planck units. Conscious states correspond to a measure-bounded subspace of this state space.

S_BH = A / (4 ℓ_p²) bits (in units of ln 2)

Consequence: there is a finite, calculable upper bound on the information required to specify any human mind. This bound is enormous but finite — and the bound is conserved (information is not destroyed) under standard physics.

5Substrate-Independent Pattern Identity

If consciousness is identified with an abstract computational/informational pattern rather than with the specific biological substrate (Chalmers, Tegmark, Tononi IIT), then the question of post-mortem existence becomes a question about the persistence of that pattern's information in the universe, not about a non-physical substance.

Pillars 1–4 establish that the pattern's information necessarily leaks out (1, 2), may be reinforced by quantum channels (3), and is conserved subject to a holographic bound (4). The conjunction is what we call the Persistence claim:

∀ mind M instantiated in brain B, ∃ pattern P(M) such that I(P(M); Environment_after_B_dies) > 0

What the hypothesis does not claim: that the environmental residue is conscious, experiences anything, or can be "reactivated" without specialized technology approaching arbitrary recovery capability. We claim only non-zero mutual information persistence — a much weaker, but testable, claim.

Falsifiable Predictions

  • P1. Inter-brain EM correlation above chance Two electromagnetically shielded human subjects, asked to perform synchronized cognitive tasks, will exhibit cross-correlated MEG/EEG signatures above chance level when shielding is partial, scaling with shielding attenuation. Test: Replicate & extend Standish et al. (2004); explicit pre-registration; classical EM coupling controls. Null result at high sensitivity falsifies P1.
  • P2. Brain-EM information channel capacity ≥ 1 bit/sec (far-field) Under ideal conditions, brain-originated EM radiation carries Shannon-extractable information about cognitive state to a far-field detector at a rate of at least 1 bit/sec. Test: Highly sensitive SQUID arrays at multiple distances; classifier trained on cognitive task labels; channel capacity computed. Null = falsifies P2.
  • P3. Bekenstein bound applied to brain is non-vacuous The calculable Bekenstein bound for a typical human brain exceeds the Tononi IIT (or any other functionalist measure) integrated-information capacity of a conscious moment by at least 6 orders of magnitude — meaning consciousness is informationally "cheap" relative to its substrate's capacity. Test: Compute both quantities under multiple assumption sets. If consciousness saturates the bound, the hypothesis loses one of its degrees of freedom.
  • P4. Quantum coherence in Posner molecules ≥ 10⁵ s in vivo Fisher's prediction: ³¹P nuclear spin coherence in posner molecules survives at biologically relevant timescales inside neurons. Test: NMR-based in vitro and ex vivo coherence measurements. Active research field; results expected within current decade. Null = Pillar 3 collapses (Pillars 1–2 still stand).
  • P5. Information-theoretic survival is decision-theoretically negligible Even if Pillars 1–4 are validated, the recoverable mutual information about a specific deceased mind decays as I(t) ~ I₀ · e^(−t/τ) with τ on the order of EM dispersion timescales in the ISM. Practical recoverability collapses within centuries. Test: Theoretical bounds on signal-to-noise vs. ISM/CMB background. If decoherence is fast, this hypothesis offers a vanishing form of "afterlife", not a robust one. Honesty requires stating this up front.

Honest Caveats

What this v2 explicitly abandons from v1

  • The "0.01% leakage" figure. It had no physical basis. Replaced with calculable thermodynamic and EM emission bounds.
  • Calabi–Yau manifolds as a literal substrate. Extra-dimensional coupling at biological energy scales is excluded by current LHC results. Removed entirely.
  • "Spirit world" terminology. Replaced with "informational pattern persistence". The reformulation makes weaker but defensible claims.
  • The projector metaphor as literal mechanism. Retained only as expository analogy, no longer load-bearing.

What this v2 still cannot do

  • It does not show that the environmental residue is conscious. Pattern persistence ≠ experiential continuity. Possibly the latter requires substrate.
  • It does not provide a mechanism for "recovery" of a deceased mind. P5 in fact argues this is practically impossible.
  • It is not yet a quantitative theory. Pillars 1, 2, 4 use only order-of-magnitude estimates.
  • It remains compatible with strict physicalism — and therefore offers no metaphysical comfort beyond what physicalism already provides.
